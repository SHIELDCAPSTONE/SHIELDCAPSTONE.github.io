An automated system designed to protect homes and communities during flood events, with special attention to vulnerable populations.

Problem Statement

S.H.I.E.L.D. (Smart Home Integrated Emergency Level-Monitoring & Disconnection System) addresses a critical safety challenge: flooding is one of the most common natural disasters globally, and when water contacts electrical systems, the combination creates significant hazards--particularly for residents who cannot respond quickly due to mobility limitations, age, or other factors. This risk is disproportionately borne by people with disabilities, older adults, low-income households, and residents of flood-prone communities.

Many existing safety systems depend on manual intervention, external power, or centralized services that may fail during emergencies. An automated, locally-operated system that remains functional without internet connectivity can meaningfully reduce both the likelihood and severity of electrical hazards during flood events.

Why It Matters: Community & Accessibility

Floods are among the most frequent weather-related disasters globally, and their impact on safety is not uniform. Approximately 1 in 6 people worldwide live with some form of disability, and during emergencies, these individuals face compounded risks due to limited mobility, communication barriers, and reduced access to timely information. Automated safety systems can reduce reliance on rapid manual response and operate independently of external infrastructure--ensuring protection reaches those who need it most.

How It Works

S
Continuous Monitoring

Dual sensors (ultrasonic and float switch) monitor water level in real time.

C
Data Processing

ESP32 validates sensor data to ensure accuracy and reduce false triggers.

A
Tiered Alerts

Progressive alerts (Warning -> Critical) trigger buzzer and SMS for early response.

R
Power Disconnection

At critical water level, a relay disconnects electrical power from protected circuits.

The system is designed to function autonomously during emergencies, reducing dependence on manual intervention when rapid response may not be possible.

Design Principles

  • Offline-First Operation: All critical decisions are made locally on the ESP32 without requiring internet connectivity.
  • Independent Local Control: Sensor data is processed and decisions are executed on the device itself for low latency and reliability.
  • Resilience During Emergencies: Core detection and disconnection remains operational 24/7 regardless of network outages.

Dashboard & Configuration

The built-in ESP32 dashboard provides real-time monitoring and configuration without requiring code changes:

Monitoring Features

  • Live Water Distance Display: Real-time sensor readings showing current water distance from the device.
  • System State Indicator: Clear display of operational status--Normal, Warning, or Critical.
  • Water Level Trends: Historical data and trend analysis to estimate time to critical threshold.
  • Activity & Alert Log: Complete record of recent system events for audit, troubleshooting, and situational awareness.

Configuration & Maintenance

Non-technical users can configure the system directly via the dashboard:

  • Alert Recipients: Add or modify phone numbers and identifiers for SMS notifications.
  • Sensor Calibration: Adjust thresholds and baseline measurements for the specific installation environment.
  • Alert Levels: Define warning and critical water level thresholds appropriate for each deployment location.
  • Wi-Fi Setup: Manage network connection and configuration settings.

Designed for Accessibility: The dashboard prioritizes clarity and simplicity, enabling caregivers, family members, and community volunteers--regardless of technical background--to monitor and maintain the system effectively.

Safety & Reliability

  • Grace Period Before Shutdown: Configurable delay provides time for system verification and coordination before power disconnection.
  • Manual Override: Physical switch allows authorized personnel to manually control or bypass the system for maintenance.
  • Dual-Sensor Validation: Ultrasonic and mechanical float switch provide independent confirmation of water level.
  • Continuous Local Operation: Core safety functions remain operational throughout internet outages and service failures.
  • Fail-Safe Design: Relay defaults to the safe state; any single point of failure does not compromise the primary mechanism.

Affordability & Practical Deployment

S.H.I.E.L.D. is built using low-cost, widely available components selected for reliability, ease of sourcing, and local repair capability. The prototype cost is approximately Php 1,000-1,500, making deployment feasible for residential homes, community centers, small facilities, and nonprofit organizations serving vulnerable populations. The simplicity of the design enables local maintenance and adaptation, avoiding dependence on specialized expertise or expensive service contracts. This approach to affordability and accessibility ensures that flood protection can scale to reach communities historically underserved by expensive, proprietary systems.
